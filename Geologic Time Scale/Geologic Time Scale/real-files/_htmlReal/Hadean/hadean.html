Hadean Eon

4,560 - 4,000 Ma

Environment

Mean temperature: 70-2100 °C

Atmospheric oxygen: 0%

Description

Hadean Eon – the earliest part of the Earth history, which covers times from the beginning of the formation of the Earth until the formation of the tectonic plates 4.0 billion years ago. The landscape of the Early Hadean looked like hell. The surface was covered by volcanoes and molten rock, and the planet was constantly attacked by meteorites and comets. The first 100 million years were too inhospitable for any kind of life, with surface temperatures of 230 degrees celsius (at the beginning even 2000 degrees), and atmospheric pressure 27 times higher than now. Earth possessed an atmosphere, but it was repeatedly lost because of frequent collisions with other space objects. 4.4 billion years ago planet began to cool; zircon discoveries provide evidences of liquid water during that time. The end of this eon is marked by the formation of the oceans and continental crust.

Life

There is no hard proof that any life form could exist in such a harsh environment; however there are 4.1-billion-year-old findings of carbon minerals in Western Australia, which provide evidence that there could have been sustainable conditions for life. Some scientists think that life may have arisen multiple times, but was repeatedly wiped out in frequent cataclysms. The main source of organic particles – carbon meteorites – may provide evidence that life exists outside of Earth.
