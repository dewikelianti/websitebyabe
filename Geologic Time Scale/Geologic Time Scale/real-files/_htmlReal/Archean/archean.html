Archean Eon

4,000 - 2,500 Ma

Environment

Mean temperature: 45-70 °C

Atmospheric oxygen: 0%

Description

Archean Eon – the second huge eon in the Earth history, which covers times from the formation of the first tectonic plates 4.0 billion years ago until the Great Oxidation Event 2.5 billion years ago. At the beginning of this eon, Earth’s temperature was 2-3 times higher than the today. The causes of this phenomenon were: high concentration of greenhouse gases (tens of times higher than today) and heat from the formation and growth of the planet core. At that time Sun had about 70–75% of the present luminosity. By the end of this eon, Earth’s tectonics were similar to the present, and there were vast oceans of liquid water.

Late Heavy Bombardment

There is no hard proof that any life form could exist in such a harsh environment; however there are 4.1-billion-year-old findings of carbon minerals in Western Australia, which provide evidence that there could have been sustainable conditions for life. Some scientists think that life may have arisen multiple times, but was repeatedly wiped out in frequent cataclysms. The main source of organic particles – carbon meteorites – may provide evidence that life exists outside of Earth.

Life

Unlike in the Hadean, the existence of life during the Archean is not in doubt, although there are disputes about the time life first appeared. The first solid evidence of life comes from the 3.85-billion-year-old banded iron formations found in Greenland. These formations require oxygen for their formation, and the only source of oxygen during that time was photosynthesis, so the existence of these formations is direct evidence of life. Possible first prokaryotes have been found in Canadian rocks that are at least 3.77 billion years old, and by judging lipid biomarkers, the first eukaryotes might have appeared by the end of the period, about 2.7 billion years ago.
